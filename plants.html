find your perfect plant

It's a common misconception that all plants are really hard to take care of. But if you choose a plant that suits your space and learn how often to water it, your plant will be thriving in no time!

Scroll down to read about the various options, and then use the buttons below to filter the selection of plants based on what best suits you and your space.

sun-lovers

need at least 6 hours of direct or indirect sunlight per day, and are best suited to bright sunny rooms.

shade-lovers

need some indirect light but are quite shade-tolerant, they like to be placed away from sunny windows.

humidity-lovers

are best suited to rooms with higher humidity such as bathrooms or conservatories, or near a humidifier.

easy-care

are hardy plants which are best suited to first-time plant parents, these can survive in a variety of conditions with minimal attention.

Use these buttons to view plants based on their different needs.

monstera

likes a semi-shaded spot with some bright indirect light

water every 1-2 weeks, when the top 2 inches of soil feel dry

calathea

likes moderate shade with a bit of bright indirect light

water lightly once a week, or when top 2 inches of soil feel dry

syngonium

likes a lightly shaded spot with some bright indirect light

water every 1-2 weeks, when the top 2 inches of soil feel dry

tradescantia

loves a bright spot that's just out of direct sunlight

water every 1-2 weeks, when the top 2 inches of soil feel dry

fishbone cactus

loves lots of bright light, but not too much direct sunlight

let soil fully dry out between light waterings every few weeks

golden pothos

fine with any light, but grows faster with some bright indirect light

water lightly once a week, or when top 2 inches of soil feel dry

snake plant

very shade-tolerant, but grows faster with some bright light

water every few weeks, once soil has almost completely dried out

fiddle-leaf fig

loves a bright spot that's just out of direct sunlight

water every 1-2 weeks, when the top 2 inches of soil feel dry

peace lily

likes a lightly shaded spot with some bright indirect light

water when the leaves look quite droopy, about once a week

anthurium

likes a spot that gets lots of bright light, but no direct sunlight

likes to be moist but not soggy, water when top 2 inches of soil feels dry

fittonia

loves a bright spot that's just out of direct sunlight

likes to be moist but not soggy, water when very top of soil feels dry

peperomia

likes a spot that gets lots of bright light, but no direct sunlight

water every 1-2 weeks, letting soil dry out between waterings
